Public Speaking

  • Whether it is a college fest, class presentation, startup idea, inter-college competition, seminar, conference, or an open forum, the art of speaking publically is important to convey your message before a potential audience.

  • Words have the power to change, inform, entertain, engage, persuade and educate the masses. It plays a major role in business, education, and across all positions at some point or the other in your life.So why not start early and develop it as a unique skill.

  • Most people when asked to speak publically and perform tend to avoid by saying they are afraid of it. It may also happen that they may be shy or introverted but in most cases, the fear is such that they don’t even consider the idea.

  • But why has it particularly become important in the recent years?
    One essential prerequisite of the business world is effective oral communication skills, which makes the art of public speaking important and essential. We operate in a world where there are hundreds of options out there giving out information easily accessible on the go. Such availability makes public speaking more important, for you to put across your thoughts and communicate effectively.

Benefits of public speaking

  Boosts your confidence

It increases your self-confidence drastically. Believe it or not, we tend to base our self-worth on what other people think about us. This should not be the only way to decide but it does affect you, right! Public speaking builds your communication skills making you confident around people automatically.

  Makes you more comfortable around people

It avoids those awkward moments and pauses when in a group and the moment you speak up showcases your leadership skills.

  Group Brings you a step closer to your aspiring job/position

Employers expect two things from fresher’s, first technical know-how and second the art of presenting yourself to the public. Speaking regularly in public makes you fluent and doesn’t appears to be a conscious effort during the job interview. You might as well land up in a higher paying job or position.
