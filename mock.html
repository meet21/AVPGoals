Mock Interviews

We often hear our seniors discussing about mock interview in their third and fourth year. I used to always wonder what would it be like, is it like an actual interview, who would be interviewing me, the very thought of an interview at times scares us.

We are here to address all such queries and give you a brief idea before you face one, and trust us it is one of the best ways to practice and learn for a student before you finally go out there and face it.

So, next time you hear out your seniors be excited ad confident that you will get to practice it one day.

Mock Interview is an exact replica of a real job interview. The only difference between a mock and a real is that the former is a practice where you will have number of sessions to correct and assess yourself whereas in the latter you wonâ€™t get a second chance. The whole idea is to provide experience for you to identify smart answers and find where your weak spots are.

Types Of Interviews

  Behavioral Interview

The interviewer tries to gauge how have you handled situations in the past and what has been your behavior during that situation. The idea it to predict how you will handle things in the new job. Narrate incidents from your life /college experiences where you rectified a situation demonstrating a particular behavior and you were appreciated for it.

  Structured Interview

Structured interview has pre-set questions which is usually asked of each and every candidate, the answers can then be easily compared. It is usually carried out in two possible ways, either one-on-one or making every candidate fill out a questionnaire form.

  Group interview

Interview where group of students are assessed together at once as a team. The panel (usually consisting of potential manager, HR and other co-workers) present a dilemma or a situation to two groups. Each team is given some time to discuss and present how they would be handling the situation. The panel members closely observe the behaviors of all the students during discussion and presentation.
