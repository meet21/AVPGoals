A Winning Resume

Starting a resume from scratch can turn out to be a nightmare if you are not aware as to where to start from. What we generally assume that resume consists of a name, address, number, work experience, and we are sorted but what after that, and even if we make a one-pager is it good enough to sail us through.

Our resume reaches the recruiter before we even think of making an impression by meeting in person, thus creating a preconceived shadow in the recruiters ‘mind. It is all about creating an ideal situation to make an impact using the power of words and presentation in a way that it reaches the sole purpose of securing you a job interview.

It is an unstated fact that a well-written resume gives you an edge and the opportunity to showcase your career goals and relevant skills acquired to achieve those goals. However, what we take for granted in that every winning resume has something unique. What makes it unique is keeping it up to date and aligning it as per your current goal.

What is a Resume?

A Resume is like a self-made marketing tool, the sole purpose of which is to secure the interview.

The next big question is how and where to start building a resume from scratch. Of course, it is difficult, to sum up your entire career journey on a single page, one can go endlessly once they start speaking about it but it is also not easy to fit it in one page. Solution lies in asking a few questions to self, starting with Am I a fresher or an experienced person, If I have experience what relevant skills I can emphasize in relation to my experience. Fresher’s need to self-analyze skills that they already have and would want to excel in future. Identify one or two achievements that you think have made a significant mark in your life, mention areas where your expertise lie and see whether it matches your target position/company. Answer to all this will create your Resume Summary.

Common Mistakes to be avoided

Make sure to cross-check all the details mentioned in the heading, and be transparent. Writing too lengthy sentences can dilute the purpose, make them crisp and to the point Don’t highlight skills that you are not sure of or don’t want to use again in the next job .

Before writing the job description, sit back memorize and pen down all your accomplishments and learnings rather than jumping to the section directly Verify that you have supporting documents for your certification

Every time you write a resume you will discover something new about yourself, it is always a good idea to quickly glance through it once in a while and add that new discovery you feel sets you apart.
