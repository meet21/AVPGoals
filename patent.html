Patents And Projects

To simply define Projects help you bridge the gap between theoretical aspects and real world. You get projects mostly in the final year of engineering. But why do these projects only in the fourth year.
Have you ever given it a thought? Why wait and let three years pass.

Do you know: Projects can be that differentiator in your resume. Projects will establish a linkage between lack of application of knowledge in the real world throughout those years. Once you see things happening practically, you start valuing that study.

A project made and handled from scratch will also help in building your confidence to speak about the project during interview process. Moreover, do you remember everything that you would’ve studied during the lectures a few months ago? May be or maybe not. But you will definitely remember a lot of things if you have done a project on the subject or a difficult topic.

    Benefits of doing projects during study.

  • Improves your Problem-Solving skills – if you are unable to decide; no need to select a difficult topic keep it simple and basic.

  • You have a better chance of getting hired if you have done a lot of good projects.

  • For working solo start with small projects first and gradually move ahead.

  • Projects makes you better prepared to face the challenges in the industry.

  • It will also give you updated on the new trends in your field of interest and study.

    How can you leverage the advantage of patented idea/projects.

  • You will have the ownership for 20 years.

  • You have the right on that future business idea, work on it and build business.

  • Rent it to existing businesses.

  • Pitch and sell it to other company.

  • Ownership gives you the right to stop anyone from selling or misusing your idea.
